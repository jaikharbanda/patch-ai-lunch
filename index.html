This session

For most small businesses, admin eats a day or more a week: non-billable busywork, tools that don't talk to each other, context scattered everywhere. This is three practical moves to take AI from a party trick to part of how your business actually runs. Use whatever tool you have open: the moves are the same in all of them.

You don't have an AI problem. You have a context problem.
Treat AI as a team you brief, not a chatbot you quiz.
Build and test ideas in a day, not a quarter.

The three pillars

Three ways AI starts working alongside you once it has your real context. Copy the starters and try them on your own business.

1

AI as Analyst

The spreadsheets that never get looked at.

You run on spreadsheets that don't talk to each other: leads, revenue, invoices, time per project. So big decisions come down to gut feel. Connect your data, folders and email, then ask plain-English questions and get business-level clarity in minutes.

Ask your own numbers
Connect my spreadsheets, project folder and email, then answer:
- What changed this month vs last month?
- Which clients are highest effort for lowest return?
- If I want to earn £5k a month, what would I need to do?

Use only my real data. Show your working and flag anything you assumed.
2

Docs as the Database

Your turn today

AI as a teammate who already knows your business.

Your knowledge is scattered: call notes here, proposals there, client preferences in your head. So you keep redoing work. Centralise it into a few living docs and AI can draft, summarise and reply in your voice, from your real context. Output compounds.

Start your ground-truth doc
# My ground-truth context pack

## Clients
- [Client] - what they care about, their tone, do's and don'ts

## Offers
- [Offer] - what's included / excluded, price logic, positioning

## Case studies
- [Project] - the result, proof points to reuse

## FAQs
- [Question] - my standard answer

## Decisions
- [Decision] - and why (so AI stops re-litigating it)

## Templates
- Proposal / email / scope / follow-up - in my voice
3

Build the MVP in a Day

Watch this one

Stop waiting on a designer, a dev and three rounds of changes.

A landing page, an offer, a checkout or an onboarding flow used to need a designer, a developer and weeks of back-and-forth. With tools like Claude Code, Cowork or Lovable you can build a working v1 in an afternoon, then test it with real customers before spending serious money.

You don't have an AI problem. You have a context problem.

When your docs become the database and your tools become the factory, AI stops guessing and starts acting like a teammate who already knows your clients, offers and the way you work.
