This session

Most people use AI like Google: ask a question, get an answer. The real value is using it as a layer over the work you already do every week. We cover the messy middle idea, one reusable prompt pattern, five workflows you can use this afternoon, and which tool to reach for.

Stop asking AI for answers. Start handing it the messy work.
The quality jump comes from better context, not magic words.
You stay the editor. AI is the drafter.

The one prompt pattern

Works the same in ChatGPT, Copilot or Claude. Give it the situation, not magic words.

I am trying to [goal].
The audience is [who it is for].
Here is the rough material / context:
[paste notes, draft, meeting context or plan]

Please turn this into [output format].
Keep it [tone / length / style].
Separate facts from assumptions.
Tell me what is missing before giving the final version.

Five workflows for this afternoon

Each one turns a pile of messy input into something usable. Copy the prompt, swap in your own material.

1. Meeting prep in 10 minutes

Before any call where the context is scattered across emails and notes.

I have a 20-minute meeting with [person/company].
Here is what I know: [paste notes / emails / context].
Give me:
1. the five things I need to know
2. three smart questions to ask
3. likely objections or risks
4. a sensible next step if the meeting goes well

2. Messy notes to next steps

Right after a call, event or brainstorm, before the thread goes cold.

Turn these rough notes into:
- decisions made
- action items
- open questions
- people mentioned
- a short follow-up message

Do not invent anything. Mark anything unclear as unclear.

Notes:
[paste notes]

3. Write and edit without sounding like AI

For emails, proposals, LinkedIn posts, event blurbs and website copy.

Rewrite this so it sounds like a clear human email.
Keep the meaning.
Make it shorter, warmer and more direct.
Remove generic AI phrases.
End with one clear ask.

Draft:
[paste draft]

4. Stress-test a plan

Before sending a proposal, launching an offer, hiring or buying software.

Stress-test this plan.
Tell me:
- what assumptions I am making
- what could go wrong
- what the simpler version is
- what evidence I should check first
- the next practical action

Plan:
[paste plan]

5. Spot repeatable workflows

When the same admin keeps eating your week.

Here are tasks I do every week:
[list tasks]

Which ones could become:
- a checklist
- a reusable prompt
- a template
- a simple automation

Rank them by time saved, risk and ease of starting.

ChatGPT, Copilot or Claude?

The workflow matters more than the logo. Start with whichever you already have, then get better at giving it useful context.

Microsoft CopilotWhen the work lives inside Microsoft 365: Word, Outlook, Teams, PowerPoint, Excel.
ChatGPTWhen you want broad help with thinking, drafting, research, structure or quick experiments.
ClaudeWhen you have long documents, messy notes, careful rewriting or larger context to process.
